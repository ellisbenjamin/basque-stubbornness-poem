Basque Proverbs About Donkeys

Asto askok, lasto asko
"A lot of donkeys means a lot of hay"
Meaning: Families with many children have lots of expenses.
Astoari ezin mendeka, mendeka albardari
"Not able to take revenge on the donkey, take it out on the saddle"
Meaning: People often won't take revenge directly on someone but instead on their things, friends or family.
Oiloari, oloa, astoari, lastoa
"To the hen, oats, to the donkey, straw"
Meaning: Give appropriate things to different beings.

The Basque Character

The Basque people are known for their strong character and determination. In Basque culture, the pottoka (a native mountain horse) is described as being "as cute as they're stubborn, and will take you wherever they please."

This reflects a cultural appreciation for independence and strong will - traits that are both admired and sometimes seen as challenging.

About This Collection

While searching for the specific Basque poem about the three most stubborn things (a woman, a man, and a donkey), we've collected these traditional Basque proverbs that feature donkeys and reflect the culture's wisdom about human nature.

The Basque language (Euskera) is one of Europe's oldest languages, and its proverbs carry centuries of wisdom about life, relationships, and the natural world.
